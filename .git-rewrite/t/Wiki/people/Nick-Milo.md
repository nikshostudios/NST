# Nick Milo

## Relevance to Niksho
Creator of the **ACE framework** (Atlas / Calendar / Efforts) and the broader **Ideaverse** approach to personal knowledge management in Obsidian. His work forms the **authored half** of the Niksho second brain architecture, complementing Karpathy's compiled half.

See [[Atlas/Concepts/AI-OS]] for the hybrid and [[Atlas/Concepts/Ideaverse]] for the design pattern this whole vault is built on.

## The contributions we built on
1. **The Ideaverse.** The whole-system framing: knowledge, time, and action live in *one* unified vault organized around a Home note Launchpad with three headspaces. See [[Atlas/Concepts/Ideaverse]].
2. **Home note as Launchpad.** The single dashboard you return to whenever you're lost or starting. The folder tree mirrors the Home note, not the other way around. See [[Home]].
3. **ACE — Atlas / Calendar / Efforts.** The three headspace folders that make the Ideaverse work in practice. Atlas for what you know, Calendar for when things happened, Efforts for what you're working on.
4. **ARC — Add / Relate / Communicate.** The three verbs you do with ideas. Most people stop at Add. The vault's job is to make Relate and Communicate cheap. See [[Atlas/Concepts/ARC-Framework]].
5. **Four Intensities of Efforts.** Active / Ongoing / Simmering / Sleeping — efforts move between states over time, replacing the binary "project / done" model. See [[Atlas/Concepts/Four-Intensities-of-Efforts]].
6. **Maps of Content as active catalysts.** A MOC is not an index. It's an invitation. Every top-level folder in Niksho has an active MOC: [[Atlas/Atlas]], [[Calendar/Calendar]], [[Efforts/Efforts]]. See [[Atlas/Concepts/Maps-of-Content]].
7. **Authored over captured.** The vault reflects how you think, not what the internet says. Your own synthesis is what makes the vault yours.
8. **Claude inside Obsidian.** His workshop on using Claude directly in the vault (not as a cloud chat) as the AI tool of choice. Informed how we structured the AIOS layer.

## What we kept from Milo
- Atlas / Calendar / Efforts folder structure (unchanged in name)
- MOCs as the navigation spine for each area
- The principle that the human writes Atlas (not the AI)
- Daily / weekly / quarterly rhythm in Calendar

## What we diverged from
- Added Raw/ and Wiki/ as Karpathy-inspired layers alongside ACE
- Added AIOS/ as a glue layer of manuals and skills
- Added `mi.md` + `claude.md` for cross-tool portability (Milo's approach assumes Obsidian + Claude specifically)
