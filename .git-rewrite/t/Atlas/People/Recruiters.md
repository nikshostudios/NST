# ExcelTech Recruiters

The 10–11 people whose daily experience is the thing we're actually changing. Every design decision in the product should be testable against "does this make a recruiter's day better or not".

## Shared characteristics
- **India-based.** Except Raghav, who focuses on SG. Full team operates out of India offices or remote.
- **Their own Outlook account each.** 10 individual Microsoft Graph connections, not a shared alias. This is a hard architectural constraint, not a choice. See [[Atlas/ExcelTech/Team#Key-technical-fact]].
- **Mid-tier hardware, variable connectivity.** Which is why the product is server-rendered Jinja2 and not a React SPA. See [[Atlas/Product/Tech-Stack#Why-Python-FastAPI]].
- **Informal assignment.** Reqs are shared to the group; recruiters pick up what they can handle. The "assigned recruiters" list on each requirement is descriptive (who actually worked it), not prescriptive.
- **Paid on performance.** Placement fees fund commissions. This means the product's success is directly visible in what they earn.

## How they experience the product
- **Login lands on their dashboard.** Assigned reqs, outreach queue, candidate replies waiting.
- **Most of their day is in the Source/Screen screen and the Outreach Queue.**
- **WhatsApp stays on their phone.** We don't try to replace that channel — we don't even touch it.
- **They don't talk to clients.** Only Raja does. Recruiters working a req will never see the client's name on an outbound email.

## What the automation needs to earn from them
**Trust, specifically.** Recruiters have been burned by recruitment software before — tools that promised automation and delivered dashboards. The only way to win is to visibly save them hours of work in week one.

The success criterion from [[Atlas/Business-Model/Phase-1-ExcelTech]]: if a recruiter says "this is helping me" unprompted by week two, we're on track.

## What we watch for
- **Push-back on AI-written outreach.** If recruiters feel the drafts don't sound like them, they'll revert to writing from scratch. The Outreach agent has to personalise on recruiter voice, not just on candidate.
- **Over-automation that removes judgment.** If the Screener auto-rejects someone a recruiter would have shortlisted, they'll stop trusting the score. Every rejection must be reviewable.
- **Inbox surprise.** If APScheduler does something to their Outlook drafts without explanation, confidence dies. Every draft has a clear "generated by" marker.
