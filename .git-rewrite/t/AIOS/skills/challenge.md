# Skill: Challenge

## Purpose
Pressure-test a belief, plan, or claim by finding contradictions, counter-evidence, and shifts in thinking within the vault itself — and by steelmanning the opposite.

## Trigger
- User says "challenge this", "pressure-test X", "steelman against Y"

## Inputs
- The specific claim or plan to challenge

## Steps

1. **Restate the claim** in one sentence as you understand it. Confirm with the user if it's ambiguous.

2. **Search the vault for contradictions.** Is there anywhere in the vault — Atlas, Efforts, Calendar, Wiki — that would argue against this claim? Surface it with quotes.

3. **Search for earlier versions of the claim.** Has the thinking on this shifted before? If so, in which direction? Sometimes today's claim contradicts last quarter's plan without anyone noticing.

4. **Steelman the opposite.** Write the strongest case against the claim you can build, using outside knowledge where the vault is silent. Explicitly mark vault-sourced vs model-generated critiques.

5. **Test against the first principles in [[mi.md]].** Does the claim violate any of the stated principles? (e.g. "ship the core, then sophisticate" might contradict "let's build the fancy dashboard first".)

6. **Produce a structured critique:**
   - **The claim as stated**
   - **Internal contradictions** — from the vault, with citations
   - **External counter-case** — from outside knowledge, marked clearly
   - **First-principle friction** — if any
   - **Where the claim is strongest** — what the vault actually supports
   - **A clarifying question** — what the human should answer before committing

## Rules & guardrails

- **Be kind but direct.** The point is to make the plan stronger, not to score points.
- **Separate vault evidence from outside critique.** Never mix them. The human should see clearly which pushback comes from their own notes.
- **Do not simply disagree.** Find reasons, or say you couldn't find any — which is itself a useful signal.
- **If the vault strongly supports the claim**, say so. Challenge isn't contrarianism. Sometimes the claim holds.
