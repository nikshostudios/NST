# This is Excel Tech’s business model 

What we built for ExcelTech Computers  
ExcelTech is a recruitment agency that connects candidates to clients. Their recruiters were spending most of their day on manual, repetitive tasks — uploading resumes, copy-pasting data into spreadsheets, writing outreach emails one by one, and managing follow-ups through a cluttered inbox.  
We built a web app that automates the most time-consuming parts of their workflow.

What it does:  
Profile Processing — Recruiters drop in resumes and the AI instantly extracts candidate details and adds them to the CRM. No manual data entry.  
AI Screening — Recruiters upload resumes alongside a job description. The AI scores each candidate out of 10 and explains why they're a match or not. Only strong candidates move forward.  
Outreach Automation — After screening, recruiters send personalised emails to matched candidates directly from the app. Emails go from their own Outlook inbox, include the JD, and ask candidates for all required details automatically.  
Email Intelligence — Recruiters connect their Outlook inbox to the app. The AI reads their emails, classifies them into categories (new requirements, candidate replies, actions needed), and suggests replies — saving hours of inbox management daily.

The bigger picture:  
Any recruitment company can implement this same approach. The core idea is simple — identify the repetitive tasks recruiters do every day, and replace them with AI agents that handle the grunt work. Recruiters then focus only on relationship building and decision making, which is what actually drives placements.  
The result is each recruiter can handle significantly more requirements simultaneously without dropping quality.

IT Recruitment — How the Business Works  
An IT recruitment agency acts as a middleman between companies that need tech talent (clients) and professionals looking for jobs (candidates).

How they make money:

Client pays the agency a fee when a candidate is successfully placed  
Fee is typically 8-15% of the candidate's annual salary for permanent roles  
For contract roles, they take a margin on the contractor's daily/monthly rate

The core workflow:

Requirement comes in — A client sends a Job Description needing a specific skill (e.g. PeopleSoft developer, Network Engineer)  
Sourcing — Recruiters search job portals (Foundit, LinkedIn) or their existing database for matching candidates  
Screening — Recruiter reviews resumes, calls candidates, checks availability, salary expectations, notice period  
Submission — Shortlisted profiles sent to client in a specific format  
Interview coordination — Client shortlists candidates, recruiter arranges interviews  
Placement — Candidate joins, agency gets paid

Where time is lost (the pain points):

Copy-pasting candidate details into trackers manually  
Writing the same outreach emails repeatedly  
Chasing candidates for missing details  
Managing follow-ups across dozens of open requirements simultaneously  
Formatting resumes to client standards  
Tracking which candidate was submitted to which client

What tools agencies typically use today:

Job portals: LinkedIn, Foundit, Naukri, JobStreet  
Tracking: Excel spreadsheets or basic ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems)  
Communication: Outlook, WhatsApp, phone calls  
Document sharing: Email attachments, Google Drive

Why AI automation is a game changer here:  
Recruitment is high volume and highly repetitive at the junior level. A recruiter might process 50-100 resumes per requirement, send 30-40 outreach emails, and manage 10-15 open requirements simultaneously. Most of that is mechanical work that AI can handle — leaving recruiters to focus on the human side: building relationships, negotiating, and closing placements.  
That's exactly what we're building for ExcelTech — and it applies to any IT recruitment agency globally.
